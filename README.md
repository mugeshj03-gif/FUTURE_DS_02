# FUTURE_DS_02
📊 Customer Retention & Churn Analysis

🧩 Problem Statement

Customer churn is one of the biggest challenges for subscription-based businesses. Losing customers directly impacts revenue and growth.

In this project, I analyzed customer data to answer key business questions:

- Why are customers leaving?
- Which customers are most likely to churn?
- How can the company improve retention?

---

🎯 Objective

The goal of this analysis is to identify churn patterns, understand customer behavior, and provide actionable recommendations to reduce churn.

---

📂 Dataset

- Telco Customer Churn Dataset (Kaggle)
- Includes customer demographics, services, account details, and churn status

---

🛠️ Tools & Technologies

- Python (Pandas, Matplotlib) / Excel


---

🔍 Approach

1. Data Cleaning

- Removed missing and inconsistent values
- Converted data types (e.g., TotalCharges to numeric)
- Checked for duplicates

2. Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

- Calculated overall churn rate
- Analyzed churn by:
   - Contract type
   - Monthly charges
   - Tenure
   - Payment method

3. Cohort & Retention Analysis

- Studied how long customers stay
- Identified patterns in customer lifecycle

---

📊 Key Insights

- 📉 Customers with month-to-month contracts have the highest churn rate
- 💰 Customers with higher monthly charges are more likely to leave
- ⏳ Customers with short tenure (new users) churn more frequently
- 🔒 Customers on long-term contracts show better retention

---

💡 Business Recommendations

Based on the analysis, the following actions can reduce churn:

- 🎯 Promote long-term subscription plans with discounts
- 🚀 Improve onboarding experience for new users
- 🎁 Introduce loyalty programs for existing customers
- 📞 Provide proactive support for high-risk customers
